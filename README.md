# Don't Panic!
## Project 2: Lost in the Scroll
[Live page](https://teliasont.github.io/scrollytelling-dont-panic-tayla/lost-in-the-scroll/index.html)
[Repo](https://github.com/teliasont/scrollytelling-dont-panic-tayla)

## Description
This project is one big metaphor for learning JavaScript and all its weird little components stylized as an interactive website. This project takes a "scrollytelling" approach, utilizing the scroll bar to make telling a story more interactive and engaging. Plus, I get to incorporate cool animations throughout. My project is centered around exploring a new world (and a new coding language).

The *lost-in-the-scroll* folder contains the index.html file, providing the structure of the webpage, the styles.css, variables.css, and the reset.css files, all which help bring life to the webpage and make it stand out, and the main.js file, which hosts all of the animations.

For this project, I wanted a unique, asymmetrical layout. I like the idea of seemingly holding the webpage in place while other elements scroll by. I thought it would be fun to have the planet on the right side of the page serve as its own scroll bar in a way, moving to different locations as your alien tour guide presents a new area. In my ideal final version, images of that area would appear in the lens that drops down as if you were zooming onto the planet. However, I did not have the time to implement that before this project was due. I hope to revisit this soon and update the page with the images, as those would also add some more interactive elements and make the webpage feel even more dynamic. I would also like to integrate ScrollSmoother in at a later date, because trying to implement it the night before this was due was breaking all of my animations. I felt it was better to have the fully realized webpage without ScrollSmoother than to have ScrollSmoother and no working animations. It will just take more time and puzzle-solving to implement it at a later time.


*This project utilizes HTML, CSS, JS, GSAP, and ScrollTrigger.*

## Reflection
**Metaphor summary**

My metaphor likened learning JavaScript to exploring an alien planet. It's full of weird wacky things that don't make a lot of sense until they do. But then they still don't. Sometimes you get the gist of what is happening, but other times it's all bogus. Sometimes you feel like you are starting to know the way, only to stumble into unexplored territory and get lost all over again. Also aliens are cool. Can't get aliens off the brain.


**One section you're most proud of, and why**

I like my intro! The starry parallax, the planet zooming in, the UFO arriving—it's all very nifty and I think it turned out quite well. It's more or less what I had pictured in my head. And that is always a nice feeling when you can put the idea in your head into reality.


**One technical bug you solved, and how**

I could not for the life of me get the text boxes scrolling in from the side to work. I tried a few different methods and looked into some other CodePens, but I could not get it to work how I wanted it to. I spent a while communicating with Gemini to try to convey my idea so I could get the text boxes to move in exactly as I wanted them to.


**One accessibility decision you made**

If a user prefers reduced motion, I stopped almost all of the animations on the page. The only animation in effect then would be the text boxes fading in and out instead of flying in from the side. 


**What you would improve with more time**

I would add in my pictures for the different alien biomes, but I simply do not have the time right now. I would also spend more time getting the ScrollSmoother to work, but considering that it is currently breaking all of my animations and I would have to refactor the whole thing the night before it is due, I would rather have my animations working and not have ScrollSmoother implemented.
