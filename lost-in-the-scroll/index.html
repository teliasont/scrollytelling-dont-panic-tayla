Don't Panic!

An Exploration of JavaScript

Hello Earthling!

Welcome to this side of the galaxy!

It seems your ship has stalled. Did someone spill tea on your navigation panel? It happens to me all the time.

Well, why that's being repaired, let me tell you all about the wondrous land of Ja-Vah Skrippt, located just 0.0000000426 light years to your right!

First up, and conviently within easy viewing is the Root Reflectory! Here, we grow many of our planets. Our brilliant, if perhaps slightly insane biologists are devised a way to perfectly copy any plant!


Placing any plant into the primary DataPot will copy the plant's attributes and other biological features immediately! Even something like picking a leaf from the main plant will cause a ripple effect down the entire row until every planet is missing that same leaf.

The cloning technology is quite incredible and makes food-gathering a breeze. Though I do wonder if anyone has tried to plant someone else in a DataPot. That's besides the point, anyways.

Just to the north a smidge is The Forest. It is where a lot of samples are gathered for the Root Reflectory.

Say, do you have forests on Earth? Do they look anything like this?

Ah, yours are a different color. But surely they function the same? Just wait for the clouds to stroll through and you'll get to see the wonder. And what great timing for the clouds, too!

Taking your expression into account, I presume this is unusual? This rather typical across Ja-Vah Skrippt. If The Forest is below a certain light level, then all the plants that thrive in low-light conditions seize the opportunity to take over The Forest.

...

This really isn't usual on your planet? How odd. I suppose we shall continue on our way.

Not too far away, if you'll just move your viewing scope, is the Triggering Hollows. Not a place I would personally recommend visiting. The place is a maze of a cave system, sprawling all over that area. It is dead quiet unless some idiot tourist stumbles into the area and begins to poke, prod, shout, or look dramatically at the caves. Any of those actions set off the residents, who burst into a raging tizzy and will scream and shout back until they've gotten it out of their system.

And if that sounds bad, then dread the thought of sneezing in front of them. The Milliways still haven't recovered.

Do you have any way of poking the hollows from your ship? Setting the residents off from space is quite amusing, and much safer.

Look at those little rascals go! I pity any tourist who wanted to get a look now. Amazing, though, isn't it, to be able to affect the planet while not even standing astride it?

Let's jump to a different area of Ja-Vah Skrippt, where the sun is just about to set. What perfect timing! You are an unusually lucky individual, I'll have you know.

Welcome to the Mirage Flats! Similar to The Forest, the flats toggle between different states. The day is hot and causes the land to turn to fine grains that slosh about. It's quite odd to walk on, like almost a liquid, but not quite. But at night! Oh, but at night it is something else! At night, the temperature drops to, quite frankly, dangerous levels and freezes the entire ground solid.

And when the land is solid, it is as reflective as any mirror you or I have ever seen! It's like having two night skys at once! How magnificent. It really helps out the Observatory with their work. They're just a few hops away from here.

Welcome to the Observatory! Though their building may look a tad beat-down on the outside, I promise you the inside is marvelous!This is the single most important building in the land of Ja-Vah Skrippt, and it is one of the greatest honors to serve as an esteemed Astronomer. The Astronomers observe the night sky (or the Mirage Flats) with its many different stars, planets, and stray flying objects to determine the appearence of the planet.

Currently, they have decreed that the stars proclaim everything should only be shown in shades of purple, pink, yellow, orange, and green, in case you were wondering why you had only seen five colors on this tour. The Astronomers have wonderful technology that lets them interface with the whole planet to present a coherent look! Though those pesky fashion industrialists are always trying to break in to get information on the future aesthetic changes.

The Astronomers have gotten very good at diverting the fashion spies away from the Observatory. Often, a shiny object thrown in the wrong direction will do the trick.

Look there! An Astronomer is updating something as we speak!

I suppose my ship lights are pink and green now, instead of orange and green. I was hoping for a more drastic shift in appearence, personally. One can dream for the stars to align next time.

I think I saw something beep behind you. It appears your navigator has been fixed! No need to make any excuses about staying. We've seen everything with even a modicum of interest. Though I do hope you get lost in this arm of the galaxy again. It would be lovely to give you a ground tour.

Do be sure to teach the other Earthlings about Ja-Vah Skrippt! Perhaps they will choose to follow in your footsteps and spill some tea on their navigator so they can experience Ja-Vah Skrippt for themselves! So long!

Thanks for checking this out!
